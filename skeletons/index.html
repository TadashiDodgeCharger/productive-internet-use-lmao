The Skeleton

The skeleton serves as the internal framework for the body of some animals. This framework consists of many individual bones and cartilages.

Bones

Bones protect the various other organs of the body, produce red and white blood cells, store minerals, provide structure and support for the body, and enable mobility. Bones come in a variety of shapes and sizes and have a complex internal and external structure.They are lightweight yet strong and hard, and serve multiple functions.

Cartilage

Cartilage is a resilient and smooth type of connective tissue. Because of its rigidity, cartilage often serves the purpose of holding tubes open in the body. Cartilage does not contain blood vessels or nerves.
